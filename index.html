Information

Video editing is the process in which a person uses a variety of recordings or raw footage and produces it all into one video. With video editing, you can plan and change whatever to your liking. Similar to art and music, there are numerous ways to come up with something creative, beautiful, innovative, etc. All movies, YouTube videos, TV shows, trailers/advertisements require editing to make your viewing experience great. Without it, you would be bored out of your mind watching monotonous content; videos won't give you that hype, rush of adrenaline, or inspiration. Because of the rapid growth and advancement of technology, video editing has become much more accessible to consumers. As a result, many content creators/videographers create stunning videos for us to view and enjoy.

Favored Video Plugins

Now you have reached the section in which editing is a lot more than combining clips and calling it a day. Here, you'll become familiar with the wide range of plugins that you can install to help broaden your options when it comes to adding a particular effect, transition, or if you want to up the overall quality of your content.

Popularly Used Effects from Boris

  • s_shake - Adds impact or heavier motion to clips.
  • s_flicker - Applies flickering to the clip for better expression.
  • s_glow - Produces glow for brightening certain areas as emphasis.
  • s_timeslice - Makes different segments of frames as a ghosting effect.
  • BCC Tritone - Allows the user to make changes to the colors of clips.
  • BCC Warp - Creates a wavy-like distortion effect.
  • BCC Directional Blur - Generates blur depending on whatever the user set the direction.
  • BCC Magic Sharp - Increases image detail for better quality.
  • BCC Swish Pan - Adds a swiping motion blur on the clip for smooth transitions.
  • BCC Scanline - Produces a VHS look to the clip.
  • BCC Prism - Makes RGB colors on objects a.k.a chromatic aberration.
  • BCC Lens Blur - Imitates camera lens bluring the subject.
  • s_invert - Generates opposite colors of the clip (i.e., black colors would be white instead).
  • s_blurmocurves - Allows for smooth transitions between clips in various directions.
  • s_rays - Creates appealing rays from light sources on the clip.
  • s_autopaint - Makes objects on the video seem as if it was painted.
  • s_kaleido - Emulates a kaleidoscope pattern.
  • s_distortrgb - Warps the RGB colors on the screen.
  • s_dissolveglintrainbow - Produces a colorful and bright glint.

Elements

Essential Categories

  1. FLOW

Transitions between shots, pan/crop, pacing (speed of the edit matching with the speed of the song), syncing in the right areas.

  1. CINEMATOGRAPHY

Were the shots aesthetically pleasing? Are the subjects within the frame (not cut off)? Do the shots follow the 180 Degree Rule and/or the Rule of Thirds? Does the motion of the shots match well with the song choice (if there isn't any, was it for good reason)? Was there any variation of angles (high and low shots)?

  1. SOUND & VISUAL EFFECTS (SFX / VFX)

Were the sounds too loud or quiet? Was the SFX not repetitive? Did the overall sound design fit the mood. Were the visuals engaging/alluring? Was it overdone or was it inadequate (too much going on or too empty-feeling)? Did it make sense along with the other elements in the video?

  1. COLORING

The choice of colors are highly dependent on what song that you choose. If conventional color norms are broken, does it improve or worsen the edit? Are the highlights, mids, and darks balanced to the extent where they are bearable/appropriate (highlights blown out, darks crushed)? Has the quality decreased because of extreme colors?

  1. EXECUTION

The ability to implement your ideas into the edit and making it work (also: whether the video looks professionally made). Having the plans is one thing, putting it all together/accomplishing it is another.

  1. FURTHER NOTES

One of the primary attributes mentioned in the categories above is how they should correlate to the song. This is vital in edits because songs are the foundation. Viewers rely on what they see and/or hear. As humans, when these senses collide/conflict, it would not feel right.

Importance of Creativity

Imagination always comes first before tackling an edit. Too different is often random/too similar is often stale. The highest form of creativity is being able to balance chaos and order. It is essential to come up with unique ideas, but if they do not make sense, it would not look pleasing. There needs to be some form of direction in edits, meaning, it should not be all over the place. Remember that creativity does not only exist in the visual effects (VFX) domain. SFXs, cinematic techniques, flow, atmosphere, are equally as necessary for creativity. Here are some questions to consider:

  • Does the edit contain underlying reasons behind the effects?
  • (Again: this has been mentioned several times, but it is crucial) Does your intention with these ideas have any connection to the song choice?
  • Do the effects blend well with the overall scene?
  • Is everything presentable, refined, polished?
